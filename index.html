Day 1 — Reykjavík
Sun Jun 7
Dinner
Matur og Drykkur ⭐ — SELECTED
Old harbour. À la carte — skip the full tasting menu on an arrival day. Salt cod, lamb, skyr dessert are the standouts. Book ahead at maturogdrykkur.is.
Known for: Salt cod head, whole roasted lamb, historic Icelandic recipes reinterpreted
~$110 CAD pp
Grillmarkaðurinn (The Grill Market)
Wood-fired meats and seafood. Tasting menu takes you around Iceland one dish at a time. Beautiful room, easier to book than Dill. Book at grillmarkadurinn.is.
Known for: Dry-aged lamb, langoustine, puffin, wood-fired everything
~$150 CAD pp
Fiskfélagið (Fish Company)
Near the old harbour. Icelandic seafood with global influences — tandoori monkfish, sushi-style arctic char. Intimate, maritime-themed. Book at fishcompany.is.
Known for: Creative seafood, fusion Icelandic-Asian dishes, extensive wine list
~$130 CAD pp

Wood-fired Icelandic lamb, langoustine, and beef. Tasting menu takes you around Iceland one bite at a time. Similar quality to Dill at roughly half the price. Book ahead at grillmarkadurinn.is.

Iceland's first Michelin-starred restaurant. Nordic tasting menu, 5 or 7 courses. Book months ahead at dillrestaurant.is. The prestige pick if budget isn't a concern.
Known for: First Michelin star in Iceland, hyper-seasonal Nordic cuisine, intimate 30-seat dining room
~$250 CAD pp
Kol Restaurant
Swanky date-night restaurant on Skólavörðustígur. Icelandic fusion — local salmon and lamb with global techniques. The langoustine soup is exceptional. Book at kolrestaurant.is.
Known for: Langoustine soup, Icelandic fusion, excellent cocktails, good value for the quality
~$110 CAD pp
