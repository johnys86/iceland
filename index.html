Day 1 — Reykjavík
Sun Jun 7
⭐ Reykjavík Specialties — Don't Miss
Bæjarins Beztu Pylsur — The Hot Dog Stand
A tiny hot dog cart by the old harbour that's been operating since 1937. Bill Clinton ate here. Order "eina með öllu" (one with everything) — lamb hot dog, crispy onions, raw onions, ketchup, sweet brown mustard, remoulade. Iceland's most famous quick eat. Cash or card. Open late.
Known for: The Icelandic hot dog — lamb, beef, and pork. The most iconic 2-minute meal in the country.
~$8 CAD pp
Sægreifinn (Sea Baron) — Lobster Soup
Wooden shed by the old harbour. Anthony Bourdain-anointed. The lobster (langoustine) soup is the move — rich, buttery, served with bread. Skewers of fresh fish, minke whale, and arctic char available if you want more. Cash counter, communal benches.
Known for: Possibly the best lobster soup on earth. ~$22 CAD a bowl.
~$25 CAD pp
Brauð & Co — Cinnamon Buns
The graffiti-covered bakery on Frakkastígur with a line out the door at 9am. Sourdough cinnamon rolls (kanelsnúður) the size of your fist. Grab one walking up to Hallgrímskirkja — they're meant to be eaten warm and immediately.
Known for: Cardamom buns, chocolate-rye sourdough, the best bakery in Iceland
~$10 CAD pp
Lunch
Messinn (Lækjargata) — Pan-Fried Fish
The lunch fish-pan special is genuinely a top-three lunch in Reykjavík. Arctic char, cod, or plaice arrives sizzling in a cast iron pan with butter, potatoes, and salad. Cheap by Icelandic standards. Walk-in usually fine at lunch.
Known for: The signature pan-fried "fiskur" — arctic char with honey-mustard sauce is the order
~$35 CAD pp
Hlemmur Mathöll — Food Hall
Reykjavík's best food hall at the east end of Laugavegur. Skál! (Nordic small plates), Flatey Pizza (volcanic-stone pizza), Bánh Mí Núdluhús, and Kröst all under one roof. Great if you and Neha want different things. No reservations — just show up.
Known for: Skál!'s arctic char tartare, Flatey's pizza, the best lunch flexibility in the city
~$30 CAD pp
Íslenski Barinn (The Icelandic Bar)
If you want to try the weird stuff in one sitting: fermented shark (hákarl), puffin, whale, smoked lamb, ram's testicles. The "Around Iceland" sampler plate is the bucket-list lunch order. Pub-style, central.
Known for: Hákarl shot with Brennivín schnapps — the full Bourdain experience
~$55 CAD pp
Dinner
Matur og Drykkur ⭐ — SELECTED
Old harbour. À la carte — skip the full tasting menu on an arrival day. Salt cod, lamb, skyr dessert are the standouts. Book ahead at maturogdrykkur.is.
Known for: Salt cod head, whole roasted lamb, historic Icelandic recipes reinterpreted
~$110 CAD pp
Grillmarkaðurinn (The Grill Market)
Wood-fired meats and seafood. Tasting menu takes you around Iceland one dish at a time. Beautiful room, easier to book than Dill. Book at grillmarkadurinn.is. Note: saved for Day 9.
Known for: Dry-aged lamb, langoustine, puffin, wood-fired everything
~$150 CAD pp
Fiskfélagið (Fish Company)
Near the old harbour. Icelandic seafood with global influences — tandoori monkfish, sushi-style arctic char. Intimate, maritime-themed. Book at fishcompany.is.
Known for: Creative seafood, fusion Icelandic-Asian dishes, extensive wine list
~$130 CAD pp
Sumac Grill + Drinks
Middle-Eastern-meets-Icelandic on Laugavegur. The lamb is exceptional. Lively, modern, easier to book than the top-tier spots. Great cocktails. Book at sumac.is.
Known for: Wood-fired Icelandic lamb shoulder, harissa-spiced cod, the best non-traditional dinner in the city
~$95 CAD pp
ÓX (Michelin)
11-seat counter inside Sümac. Two Michelin stars (as of 2024). Tasting menu only, books out months ahead. The most exclusive table in Iceland. Save this for a return trip — almost impossible to land last-minute.
Known for: 11-seat chef's counter, hyper-local Icelandic tasting, two Michelin stars
~$400 CAD pp

Day 2 — Golden Circle
Mon Jun 8
⭐ Golden Circle Specialty Stops
Efstidalur II — Farm Ice Cream
Working dairy farm between Geysir and Gullfoss. Ice cream made from the cows you can see through the window of the parlour. The skyr ice cream is the move. Also a restaurant if you want to upgrade lunch. 5 min detour off the route.
Known for: Farm-fresh skyr ice cream, looking the cow in the eye who made your dessert
~$12 CAD pp
Lunch
Friðheimar Farm ⭐ — SELECTED
Geothermal greenhouse tomato restaurant between Þingvellir and Geysir. Sounds gimmicky, becomes a trip highlight. Book ahead at fridheimar.is — fills up.
Known for: Tomato soup, Bloody Mary bar, homemade bread, eating inside a working greenhouse
~$60 CAD pp
Lindin Restaurant — Laugarvatn
On Lake Laugarvatn, between Þingvellir and Geysir. Game-focused — reindeer carpaccio, smoked goose, arctic char. The chef is famous in Iceland. Smaller and more refined than Friðheimar. Book at lindin.is.
Known for: Reindeer carpaccio, smoked Arctic char, lake views, chef Baldur Öxdal
~$70 CAD pp
Geysir Glíma Restaurant — Bread Baked in the Earth
At Laugarvatn Fontana spa (10 min from Geysir). The geothermal rye bread is baked underground in the hot sand by the lake — they pull it out steaming. Soup-and-bread lunch is the move. Tour times available too.
Known for: Hverabrauð — rye bread cooked in the earth using volcanic heat, served with smoked trout and butter
~$50 CAD pp
Dinner
Hotel Geysir Restaurant — SELECTED
On site at the hotel — Icelandic lamb, fresh fish, good wine list. After a long driving day, staying in is the right call. Book when reserving the hotel.
Known for: Icelandic lamb, views of the geothermal field, convenience
~$90 CAD pp
Gullfoss Kaffi
Casual cafeteria-style at Gullfoss. Famous lamb soup (kjötsúpa) — refillable, hearty, and the perfect post-waterfall warm-up. Closes at 9pm in summer. Drop-in only.
Known for: Bottomless lamb soup with rye bread, sheep-spotting views from the windows
~$40 CAD pp

Cosy log cabin café/restaurant in Vík. Local lamb burgers, pizza, langoustine pasta. Great lunch stop after Reynisfjara. Lively atmosphere, frequently packed. Walk-in works but expect a 15 min wait at peak.
Known for: Lamb burgers, langoustine pizza, the only proper sit-down spot in Vík

Gamla Fjósið (The Old Cowhouse) — Hvolsvöllur
Working farm turned restaurant on Route 1, 30 min before Seljalandsfoss. They serve their own beef — the "Volcano Soup" (slow-braised beef stew) is legendary. Rustic, family-run, no reservations needed.
Known for: "Volcano Soup" — beef stew from their own herd, slow-cooked for 8 hours
~$40 CAD pp
Smiðjan Brugghús — Vík
Craft brewery in Vík with excellent burgers and a tap list of Icelandic beers. Loud, fun, after-the-beach atmosphere. The Smoked Black Sand burger (smoked cheese, bacon jam) is the order.
Known for: Their own craft beer, smash burgers, Vík's only brewery
~$45 CAD pp
Dinner 🎂
Hotel Jökulsárlón Restaurant — SELECTED
On site at the hotel. Birthday dinner next to a glacier lagoon — order the lamb and a good bottle of wine. The setting does most of the work.
Known for: Glacier views, Icelandic lamb and fish, romantic setting
~$120 CAD pp
Fosshotel Glacier Lagoon — Jökla Restaurant
The other glacier hotel restaurant. Tasting menu featuring local lamb, langoustine, and arctic char with Vatnajökull glacier framing the dining room window. Good wine list. Heads up if you switch hotels.
Known for: Glacier views, slow-cooked lamb, arctic char
~$110 CAD pp
Systrakaffi — Kirkjubæjarklaustur
If you arrive earlier than expected, this is the proper sit-down spot between Vík and the glacier lagoon — pizza, pasta, fish dishes. Cosier than the hotel restaurants. Casual, no reservations.
Known for: Reindeer pizza, char-grilled Icelandic lamb chops
~$60 CAD pp

Day 4 — Höfn
Wed Jun 10
⭐ Höfn Specialty
Höfn is Iceland's Langoustine Capital
The town is literally surrounded by langoustine fishing waters — every restaurant in town has langoustine on the menu and it's all caught that morning. There's a langoustine festival here every July. Eat at least one langoustine meal in Höfn. Two if you can.
Known for: Humar (langoustine/Norway lobster) — sweeter, more delicate than maine lobster
—
Lunch
Skaftafell Visitor Centre Café — SELECTED
Post-Svartifoss hike, this is the only practical lunch in Skaftafell. Cafeteria-style but the lamb soup, sandwiches, and skyr cake do the job. Quick — you have a zodiac to catch at 2pm.
Known for: Lamb soup, post-hike refuelling, being the only option
~$30 CAD pp
Hali Country Hotel — Þórbergssetur
Between Skaftafell and Jökulsárlón. Beautiful restaurant inside a literary museum (yes, really) with floor-to-ceiling glacier views. Smoked arctic char and lamb. Surprisingly excellent given the remoteness.
Known for: Smoked arctic char from the family's own smokehouse, glacier views, literary museum oddity
~$50 CAD pp
Jökulsárlón Café (the bus)
Right at the glacier lagoon car park. Soup, sandwiches, hot dogs from a stationary bus. Nothing remarkable food-wise but the view from your table is one of the world's great lunch spots.
Known for: Eating a hot dog while watching icebergs drift past
~$25 CAD pp
Dinner
Pakkhús ⭐ — SELECTED
Iceland's best langoustine. Right in Höfn harbour. Book the tasting menu — glacier views from the table. Essential. Book at pakkhus.is.
Known for: Langoustine tasting menu, Vatnajökull glacier views, being the best seafood outside Reykjavík
~$130 CAD pp
Humarhöfnin (Lobster House)
Höfn's other langoustine specialist. Slightly more casual than Pakkhús but equally good shellfish. Good fallback if Pakkhús is full. Book at humarhofnin.is.
Known for: Langoustine soup, grilled langoustine, informal harbourside dining
~$100 CAD pp
Otto Matur & Drykkur — Höfn
Newest entrant on the Höfn scene. Modern Icelandic with European technique, in a beautifully restored old warehouse. Less famous than Pakkhús which means easier to book. The langoustine ravioli is the standout.
Known for: Langoustine ravioli, slow-cooked lamb shoulder, refined modern Icelandic plating
~$110 CAD pp

Day 5 — East Iceland
Thu Jun 11
⭐ East Iceland Specialty
Reindeer Country
East Iceland is the only region where reindeer roam — descendants of 18th-century imports from Norway. Hunting season opens in July but smoked and dry-aged reindeer is on menus year-round. Almost every restaurant from Djúpivogur to Egilsstaðir serves it.
Known for: Smoked reindeer carpaccio, reindeer steak, dry-aged reindeer charcuterie
—
Lunch
Kaffi Hornið — Höfn — SELECTED
Pre-departure breakfast/lunch in Höfn before the long East Fjords drive. Pasta, fish, langoustine soup. Cosy log-cabin atmosphere. Quick service.
Known for: Langoustine pizza, lamb soup, the locals' favourite
~$40 CAD pp
Hotel Framtíð — Djúpivogur
Historic harbour-front hotel with restaurant in the old trading post. Fresh-off-the-boat fish, langoustine pasta. Perfectly timed lunch stop on the East Fjords drive — right at the Eggs of the World sculptures.
Known for: Pan-fried plaice, langoustine pasta, historic 1890 harbour building
~$55 CAD pp
Havarí — Berufjörður
Vegetarian-leaning farm café in the middle of the East Fjords. Run by a former indie musician. Sourdough pizzas from their own grain, homemade barley sausages, ridiculous coffee. Bohemian and surreal in the best way.
Known for: Barley-based "Bulsur" sausages, sourdough pizza, accidentally being a music venue some nights
~$35 CAD pp
Dinner
Hotel 1001 Nott Restaurant ⭐ — SELECTED
On site at the booked hotel. Family-run with an exceptional chef, outstanding wine list, cognac lounge with panoramic lake views. Reindeer, lamb, arctic char.
Known for: Reindeer steak, dry-aged Icelandic lamb, cognac lounge with lake views
~$110 CAD pp
Nielsen Restaurant — Egilsstaðir
Oldest house in Egilsstaðir, converted into a restaurant. Smoked goose, reindeer carpaccio, arctic char. Charming, historic, walk-in friendly. The reindeer is among the best in East Iceland.
Known for: Reindeer fillet, smoked goose breast, historic 1944 home setting
~$95 CAD pp
Vínbúðin Egilsstaðir Bar + Salt Café Bistro
More casual option in town. Salt has burgers, pizza, fish dishes — solid quality, no reservation needed. Better if you want a low-key night before the long Mývatn drive.
Known for: Reindeer burger, fish of the day, casual atmosphere
~$55 CAD pp

Day 6 — Mývatn
Fri Jun 12
⭐ Mývatn Specialty — Geothermal Rye Bread
Vogafjós Hverabrauð — Earth-Baked Rye
Vogafjós (your dinner spot) is also famous for hverabrauð — dense, sweet rye bread baked underground using geothermal heat for 24 hours. Sold for takeaway too. Eat with smoked trout and butter. Iconic Mývatn experience.
Known for: 24-hour earth-baked rye bread, served with smoked Mývatn trout and Icelandic butter
~$15 CAD pp
Daddi's Pizza — Reykjahlíð
The only pizza place in Mývatn but locals genuinely rave about it. Reindeer pizza, smoked-trout pizza, the works. Drop-in late-night option if Vogafjós doesn't work out.
Known for: Reindeer pizza, smoked-trout pizza, being weirdly excellent for a roadside slice shop
~$30 CAD pp
Lunch
Vogafjós Farm Café (lunch service) — SELECTED
Same farm as your dinner spot but the lunch service is different — soup, sandwiches, hverabrauð with smoked trout. Quick, hot, the cow-window experience without committing to a full dinner. Walk-in works at lunch.
Known for: Smoked trout on geothermal rye, lamb soup, watching the cows during your sandwich
~$35 CAD pp
Kaffi Borgir — Dimmuborgir
Café at the Dimmuborgir lava field entrance. Lamb stew, soup, cakes. Quick stop between Námaskarð and Hverfjall if you need lunch on the move. Nothing fancy but well placed.
Known for: Lamb stew, perfect timing between attractions
~$30 CAD pp
Skútustaðir Geo Restaurant
South side of Lake Mývatn near the pseudo-craters. Bigger menu — burgers, fish, pasta, soups. Quieter than Vogafjós at lunch. Lake views.
Known for: Arctic char, lamb burger, lake views from the south shore
~$50 CAD pp
Dinner
Vogafjós Farm Restaurant ⭐ — SELECTED
You can watch the cows being milked through a glass wall while you eat. Farm-to-table on the lake shore. One of the most unique dining experiences in Iceland. Book ahead at vogafjos.net.
Known for: Cows-through-the-window experience, reindeer, arctic char, homemade skyr desserts
~$100 CAD pp
Mylla Restaurant — Berjaya Mývatn
On site at the hotel. Standout restaurant for the region — local reindeer, arctic char, lamb. Good fallback if Vogafjós is full. Book at icelandhotelcollectionbyberjaya.com.
Known for: Reindeer, lake views, local North Iceland produce
~$90 CAD pp
Gamli Bærinn (The Old Farm) — Reykjahlíð
Pub-style spot in the village. Mývatn arctic char, lamb soup, decent beer list. More relaxed than Vogafjós, walk-in friendly. Great if you want a low-key night after a big day of sights and hot baths.
Known for: Mývatn arctic char, lamb soup, local Icelandic beer
~$60 CAD pp

Day 7 — Drive to Westfjords
Sat Jun 13
⭐ Akureyri Specialty Stops
Brynja Ice Cream ⭐ — SELECTED
Been going since 1939. People drive across Iceland for it. The texture is somehow halfway between soft-serve and gelato. Order the "Bragðarefur" (Iceland's version of a McFlurry — mix-ins blended into soft-serve). Non-negotiable stop.
Known for: Iceland's most famous ice cream parlour. The Bragðarefur is the order.
~$10 CAD pp
Kaffi Ilmur — Akureyri
Charming café in an old wooden house. Famous brunch buffet — Icelandic cheeses, smoked salmon, fresh bread, skyr. If you want a proper sit-down on Day 7, this is it. Adds 45 min though.
Known for: Brunch buffet with Icelandic cheeses, homemade cakes
~$40 CAD pp
Bautinn — Akureyri
Akureyri institution since 1965. Burgers, fish, classic Icelandic. Massive portions. The locals' lunch spot. If Brynja doesn't fill you up, this does.
Known for: Iceland's biggest burger menu, generous lunch portions
~$40 CAD pp
Lunch / Roadside (between Akureyri and Patreksfjörður)
Vegamót — Hvammstangi (seal capital)
~2 hrs west of Akureyri on Route 1. Casual roadside restaurant. Lamb soup, sandwiches, burgers. Hvammstangi is Iceland's seal-watching hub if you want a quick stretch and a coffee.
Known for: Lamb soup, harbour town café, sealwatching just outside town
~$35 CAD pp
Sjávarborg — Hólmavík
Halfway through the Strandir coast drive. Right by the harbour — fish soup, sandwiches, coffee. Last proper food stop before Patreksfjörður. About 3.5 hrs from Akureyri, 3 hrs to go.
Known for: Fjord-fresh fish soup, the only café for an hour in either direction
~$35 CAD pp
Pack a picnic from Akureyri Krónan
Honestly the most efficient call given the drive. Krónan supermarket — flatbread (flatkökur), smoked lamb (hangikjöt), Icelandic cheeses, skyr, pönnukökur pancakes. Eat at any fjord pullover. ~$30 CAD for both of you.
Known for: Hangikjöt (smoked lamb) on flatbread is Iceland's defining picnic sandwich
~$15 CAD pp
Dinner
Restaurant Skel — Fosshotel Westfjords — SELECTED
On site at the hotel. Local produce, good wine list. After 7.5 hrs of driving, staying in is the only sensible call.
Known for: Local fish and lamb, Westfjords produce, convenience
~$80 CAD pp
Stúkuhúsið — Patreksfjörður
Bistro in a historic building right in town, 5 min from the hotel. Cosy atmosphere, local fish, lamb burger. Better food and atmosphere than the hotel — only skip it if you're too wrecked to leave.
Known for: Local cod, lamb burger, historic 1922 building, the town's best restaurant
~$70 CAD pp
Albína — Patreksfjörður
Tiny harbour-side bistro/bar. Fresh local catch, casual, a bit hipster. Often has live music on weekends. Walk-in friendly.
Known for: Daily fresh catch, craft beer selection, harbour views
~$60 CAD pp

Day 8 — Westfjords
Sun Jun 14
⭐ Westfjords Specialty
Harðfiskur — Wind-Dried Fish
The Westfjords is the harðfiskur capital of Iceland — cod or haddock dried in the salt wind for months until jerky-like. Pick some up from any village shop. Eat it with butter. Tastes like the ocean. Don't skip.
Known for: Iceland's oldest snack — wind-dried cod, eaten with butter. Travels home well.
~$10 CAD pp
Beach Café / Late Breakfast
Franska Kaffihúsið — Rauðasandur Beach — SELECTED
Tiny café right on the beach. Icelandic waffles, cakes, and coffee while you watch seals. One of the most surreal café locations in the world.
Known for: Icelandic waffles with jam and cream, being in the middle of nowhere
~$15 CAD pp
Lunch (between Rauðasandur and Látrabjarg)
Hnjótur Museum Café — SELECTED
Tiny café at the eccentric Hnjótur folk museum on the road between Rauðasandur and Látrabjarg. Soup, sandwiches, coffee. Only proper food stop on the loop. The museum itself (vintage cars, planes, fishing gear, a literal crashed Navy plane in the field) is wonderfully strange.
Known for: Westfjords fish soup, harðfiskur snacks, the crashed Navy plane outside
~$30 CAD pp
Just eat more at Franska Kaffihúsið
Honestly viable. The café does substantial enough food (waffles, soup, fish-of-the-day depending on the day) that you could call breakfast lunch and skip the second stop. The setting is the meal.
Known for: Eating the same waffle twice because the view's that good
~$25 CAD pp
Back to Patreksfjörður — Stúkuhúsið lunch
If the loop is faster than expected, head back to Patreksfjörður for a proper sit-down lunch at Stúkuhúsið (Day 7 dinner option). Lamb burger, local fish, charming room.
Known for: Local cod, lamb burger, town's best restaurant
~$50 CAD pp
Dinner
Restaurant Skel — Fosshotel Westfjords — SELECTED
Second night on site. Local produce, good wine list. Early night — tomorrow starts at 7am.
Known for: Local Westfjords fish, lamb, on-site convenience
~$80 CAD pp
Heimsendi — Patreksfjörður
Local seafood restaurant in town, 5 min from the hotel. More casual than Skel. Great fresh fish caught from the fjord the same day.
Known for: Fresh local catch, casual atmosphere, good value
~$60 CAD pp
Stúkuhúsið — Patreksfjörður
If you did the hotel dinner on Day 7, swap to Stúkuhúsið tonight for variety. Historic 1922 building, town's best food, 5 min walk from the hotel. Charming, local, much better than a second hotel dinner.
Known for: Local cod, lamb burger, historic 1922 building
~$70 CAD pp

Day 9 — Ferry + Reykjavík
Mon Jun 15
⭐ Stykkishólmur Specialty (post-ferry)
Narfeyrarstofa — Stykkishólmur

Sjávarpakkhúsið — Stykkishólmur Tapas
Modern seafood tapas restaurant in the old salt-fish packing house. The scallop and mussel small plates are the move if you stop here instead of eating on the ferry.
Known for: Scallop ceviche, mussel pots, refined seafood small plates
~$60 CAD pp
Lunch (on the ferry)

Driving — important rules
Speed limit: 90 km/h paved, 80 km/h gravel. Headlights on at all times (mandatory). Never drive off-road — illegal and a $5,000+ fine. F-roads require 4x4 — your Defender qualifies.
Reynisfjara Black Sand Beach
⚠️ Sneaker waves kill people every year. Stay 30m+ from the waterline at all times. Not negotiable.
Midnight Sun — June
It never gets fully dark. Bring a sleep mask. Sunrise ~3am, sunset ~midnight. Golden light at 11pm is extraordinary — stay up for it at least once.
Packing essentials
Waterproof jacket + trousers (mandatory). Layers — weather changes fast. Swimwear for every hot spring. Wool underlayers for Silfra snorkel. Good walking shoes with ankle support.
Petrol stations
Fill up in every town — stations are sparse in the East Fjords and Westfjords. N1 and Orkan are the main chains. Card payment at unmanned pumps 24/7.
